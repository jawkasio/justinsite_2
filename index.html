Our Psychotherapists

Registered Psychotherapist (Qualifying)

Justin Appler

Justin is passionate about journeying alongside people of all ages, races, sexual orientations, and gender identities and knows first-hand the healing potential of psychotherapy. He adopts a non-pathologizing perspective and views clients as the experts on their own lives. With empathy and compassion, Justin supports clients navigating uncertainty, life transitions, and emotional challenges. He specializes in grief, trauma, anxiety, depression, life transitions, relationship difficulties, spirituality and religion, death and dying, health complications, and LGBTQ2A matters.

Registered Psychotherapist (Qualifying)

Portuguese Speaker

Rachel Alencar

Rachel supports individuals navigating neurodivergence, gender-based violence, newcomer adjustment, and trauma-related concerns, including suicidality and PTSD, offering compassionate and informed care across diverse populations. Her practice welcomes individuals (teenagers and adults), couples, and families, as they work through emotions, reconnect with themselves and their strengths, break unhelpful patterns, reduce stigma, and make meaningful, empowered choices that align with their values.

Registered Psychotherapist (Qualifying)

Mandarin Speaker

Jiayi (Jenny) Zhang

Jenny is a culturally sensitive, person-centered psychotherapist providing services in both English and Mandarin. She believes that the therapeutic relationship itself is a powerful foundation for healing. Her approach is integrative, grounded in humanistic values, and guided by evidence-based practices. She is experienced working with individuals, couples, and families with challenges in personal growth and career development, mental health, interpersonal relationships, and partner communication.

Book with Jenny

Registered Psychotherapist (Qualifying)

Lauren Vandervoort

Lauren offers a grounded, integrative approach to psychotherapy that brings together depth psychology, relational psychodynamics, and spiritually informed care. Drawing on attachment theory, psychoanalytic frameworks, and trauma-informed practice, she supports individuals navigating grief, identity shifts, spiritual questions, and life transitions. Whether you're struggling with emotional crisis, complex grief, loss, or a sense of disconnection, Lauren provides a space where unconscious patterns can surface and be gently explored.

Book with Lauren

Sarah Grey

Sarah's down-to-earth, approachable style makes her a good fit for those who find it hard to open up and be vulnerable. She believes we all have undiscovered sources of strength and resilience and she'll relate to you on a human level with warmth and compassion.
