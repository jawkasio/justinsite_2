About Us

Our psychotherapists take a compassionate and integrative approach in which clients learn to access the vast power of their inner resources.

Whether you are struggling with a major life transition, a challenging relationship, significant loss, or the pain of trauma, you can begin to come to terms with your present reality and make meaningful changes to enhance your well-being and personal growth.

Our Psychotherapists

Justin Appler (He/Him)

Registered Psychotherapist (Qualifying)

Clinical Director and Founder

Supporting: Youth (12+), Individuals, Couples and Families

Languages: English

Justin is passionate about journeying alongside his clients and knows first-hand the healing potential of therapy. With empathy, compassion, and a deep appreciation for spirituality, he supports folks navigating uncertainty, life transitions, and emotional challenges.

Justin approach to therapy is grounded in Accelerated Experiential Dynamic Psychotherapy (AEDP). This is a deeply relational trauma-informed modality which harnesses the power of the body-mind to heal by undoing aloneness through the therapeutic relationship.

Justin specializes in:

  • Anxiety
  • Stress

Lauren Vandervoort (She/Her)

Registered Psychotherapist (Qualifying)

Supporting: Individuals (18+)

Languages: English

Lauren offers a grounded, integrative approach to therapy that weaves together depth psychology, relational psychodynamics, and spiritually informed care. Her practice creates a steady, spacious environment where unconscious patterns can rise into awareness and be met with curiosity rather than judgment.

Lauren works with people moving through grief, identity shifts, spiritual tension, and major life transitions. Her approach draws from attachment theory, contemporary psychoanalytic thought, trauma-informed practice, and her lived experience as a spiritual care provider in acute care. The result is therapy that is both deeply rooted and quietly transformative—support that honours the complexity of being human.

Lauren specializes in:

  • Grief
  • Trauma
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Life Transitions
  • Relationship Difficulties
  • Spirituality and Religion
  • Death and Dying
  • Health Complications
